🚆 The Intermodal Lifecycle

Rail is for distance. Drayage is for the "First & Last Mile."

Drayage

Short distance trucking between port/rail and warehouse.

Class I Rail

Long-haul cross-country (UP, BNSF, CSX, Norfolk Southern).

Transloading

Moving cargo from ocean containers to 53' domestic trailers.

🌊 Ocean Freight & The COGSA Trap

In trucking, the carrier is liable for the full cargo value. In ocean freight, the **Carriage of Goods by Sea Act (COGSA)** limits liability to just **$500 per package**.

Example of a Disaster:

A container of high-end electronics worth **$1,000,000** falls off a ship. If you didn't buy separate Marine Cargo Insurance, the steamship line only owes you **$500**.

⚠️ Never move ocean freight without verifying All-Risk Marine Insurance.

Global Lexicon

Bill of Lading (Ocean)

Unlike a truck BOL, an Ocean BOL acts as a "Document of Title." Whoever holds the original BOL literally owns the cargo.

TEU

Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit. The standard measure for ocean shipping volume. A 40ft container is 2 TEUs.

Bridge Laws

Federal regulations (Inner Bridge Formula) that dictate how much weight can be carried on axles based on how far apart they are.

NVOCC

Non-Vessel Operating Common Carrier

A broker for ocean freight who issues their own House BOLs but doesn't actually own the ships.
