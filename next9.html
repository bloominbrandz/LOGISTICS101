⚖️ The Carmack Amendment (49 U.S.C. § 14706)

The Carmack Amendment governs interstate cargo liability. It creates a strict liability regime for carriers with only 5 statutory defenses.

Carmack's 5 Defenses (Carrier Must Prove)

  1. Act of God: Natural disasters, unforeseeable weather
  2. Public Enemy: War, terrorism, riot
  3. Public Authority: Government seizure, quarantine
  4. Inherent Vice: Natural deterioration (fruit ripening, chemical instability)
  5. Shipper Negligence: Improper packaging, faulty loading

Missing from this list: Theft, driver error, carrier negligence, accidents. Carmack makes carriers liable for these.

Carmack Liability Limits

    • Released value: $100,000 max
    • Full actual loss: If no release
    • Claim filing: 9 months
    • Lawsuit: 2 years

Broker Carmack Status

    • Pure brokers: Not carriers
    • Liability: Negligence only
    • Shipper can sue both

👁️‍🗨️ Blind Shipments: The Identity Shield

Blind shipments hide origin/destination parties from each other. Common in distributor relationships.

Logistics Glossary

BOL

Bill of Lading. Legal contract between shipper and carrier.

POD

Proof of Delivery. Signed document confirming receipt.

RC

Rate Confirmation. Broker-carrier contract.

FSC

Fuel Surcharge. Price adjustment for diesel cost.

TONU

Truck Order Not Used. Cancellation fee.

HOS

Hours of Service. Driver time limits.

ELD

Electronic Logging Device. Electronic HOS tracking.

MC#

Motor Carrier Number. Operating authority ID.

NMFC

Freight Classification for LTL pricing.

LTL

Less Than Truckload shipment.

OTD

On-Time Delivery percentage.
