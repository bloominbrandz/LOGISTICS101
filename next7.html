Module 08: Specialized Freight

Hazmat, Reefer (Cold Chain), and Flatbed Securement

☣️ Hazmat: High Risk, High Reward

Legal Reality: Criminal Penalties for Violations

[Image placeholder for Hazmat class placards (Class 1-9)]

Core Requirements:

  • Carrier must have specific Hazmat Authority.
  • Driver must have a **Hazmat Endorsement** on their CDL.
  • Trailer must be correctly **PLACARDED** on all 4 sides.
  • Shipper must provide a 24-hour Emergency Response number.

Broker Checklist:

Verify the UN Number, Packing Group, and Hazmat Class. For high-hazard classes (Class 1 or 7), require $5M+ auto liability insurance.

❄️ The Reefer (Cold Chain)

[Image placeholder of a reefer trailer unit diagram with temperature logger points]

Cold Chain Standards:

  • Continuous Temperature Monitoring is required.
  • FDA 21 CFR 11 governs electronic records (temp logs).
  • Pre-Cooling of the trailer must be confirmed.
  • "Pulping" the product at pickup ensures it starts within range.

Crucial Temp Ranges:

Pharma (35°F-46°F), Produce (32°F-55°F), Frozen (0°F-10°F), Deep Frozen (-20°F).

🔩 Flatbed: Securement Physics

[Image placeholder showing a flatbed with straps, chains, edge protectors, and coil racks securing steel coils]

Securement Rules (FMCSA 393):

  • Chains for steel/machinery (Grade 70+).
  • Straps for lumber/palletized goods (4" minimum).
  • Edge Protectors prevent cargo cutting the straps.
  • Coil Racks are required for steel coils to prevent rolling.

Liability Fact:

Drivers are 100% legally responsible for securement, but a broker can be sued for "Negligent Selection" if they hire an unvetted carrier.

The Rules of Special Ops

Carmack Amendment

Federal law that holds carriers strictly liable for cargo loss or damage unless they can prove they were not negligent.

WLL

Working Load Limit. The maximum weight a strap or chain can legally support. Used in securement physics.

ADULTERATED

FDA term for food or pharmaceuticals that are considered unsafe or spoiled because they left their cold chain parameters.

PLACARDING

[Image showing a hazmat Class 3 flammable liquid placard on a semi-trailer]

Applying one of the 9 types of official hazmat warning signs to all four sides of a trailer. **Failure to Placard = MASSIVE FINES.**
