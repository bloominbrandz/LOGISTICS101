BOL, POD, and Rate Confirmations: The Legal Trinity

01. The Document Trinity

The BOL (Bill of Lading)

The Title. It proves who owns the cargo and acts as the contract of carriage between the shipper and carrier.

LEGAL CONTRACT
The POD (Proof of Delivery)

The Receipt. Once signed by the receiver, the carrier's liability usually ends. No POD = No Payment.

PAYMENT TRIGGER
The RC (Rate Confirmation)

The Price Tag. The agreement between Broker and Carrier. Must include the MC#, rate, and any fees.

Paperwork Glossary

Clean BOL

A Bill of Lading where the carrier has noted NO damage or shortages during pickup.

SL&C

Shipper Load and Count. This protects the carrier if they weren't allowed to watch the truck being loaded.

Lumper Receipt

A receipt for third-party labor used to unload the truck. Must be attached to the POD for reimbursement.

Accessorials

Extra fees listed on the RC, like Detention ($50/hr) or Layover ($250).

SCAC Code

Standard Carrier Alpha Code. A unique 4-letter code used to identify a transportation company.
