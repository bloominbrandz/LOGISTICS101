Module 07: Compliance & Fraud

Double-Brokering, Identity Theft, and Insurance Forgery

🚨 The Double-Brokering Epidemic

Loss Estimate 2024: $500M+ annually

How the Scam Works:

  1. Scammer poses as a carrier and takes your load.
  2. Scammer re-posts your load on a public board for a higher rate.
  3. A legitimate carrier hauls the load.
  4. You pay the Scammer; the Scammer disappears without paying the driver.
  5. The legitimate carrier sues you or the shipper for payment.

Identity Red Flags:

  • • VOIP/Burner phone numbers (Check Area Codes).
  • • Gmail/Yahoo/Outlook email addresses (No professional domain).
  • • MC# less than 6 months old.
  • • 0 Inspections on FMCSA SAFER despite having "20 trucks."

The Vetting Protocol

Never Trust a PDF Certificate

Fraudsters use Photoshop to fake insurance. Follow this strict chain of verification:

1

Call the Agent: Do NOT use the number on the PDF. Google the agency's name and call their official office line.

2

Verify "Additional Insured": Ensure your brokerage is listed correctly to ensure you are notified if the policy cancels.

3

Cross-Check FMCSA: Match the address on the insurance cert with the address on the MC# authority paperwork.

Security Clearance

Compliance Tools

Carrier411

[Logo placeholder for Carrier411 service]

The industry-standard website for checking carrier fraud reports and performance history. If they have "Notes" here, read them carefully.

SAFER (FMCSA)

The Safety and Fitness Electronic Records System. Public database showing inspections, crashes, and authority status.

Contingent Cargo

Insurance that brokers carry. It only pays out if the carrier's primary insurance fails. It is NOT a substitute for carrier insurance.

TIA Watchdog

A reporting tool for the Transportation Intermediaries Association where brokers report bad-acting carriers.
